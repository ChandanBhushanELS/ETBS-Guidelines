ETBS Sensitive Question Guidelines

Introduction

Purpose: Textbook solutions represent the voice of Evelyn Learning. To be the optimal learning destination for every course, Evelyn Learning textbook solutions should be objective, fair, and accessible. Evelyn Learning does not endorse specific opinions or that cause users/students undue discomfort or are inconsistent with the company brand.

Objective: Different courses may use textbooks which include both sensitive questions. The ETBS Sensitive Question Guidelines provide instructions for identifying questions that are sensitive and reporting inappropriate solutions.

Identifying Sensitive Questions

Sensitive Questions

What is a Sensitive Question?

Sensitive questions ask about topics that cause an emotional reaction. Sensitive questions may address personal or political beliefs and can make a person feel uncomfortable.

How Do I Know if a Question is Sensitive?

Culture and age affect how topics are perceived, and affect which topics are sensitive. The primary users of Evelyn Learning Textbook Solutions are college students in the United States, and certain topics are considered sensitive for this United States culture. Solution authors should become familiar with the list of sensitive topics so that they may identify sensitive questions while working on solutions.

Sensitive questions may address one or more of the following topics. The Sensitive Topics document is an expanded list that includes sensitive issues within these topics.

  • Animal rights
  • Antisocial behavior
  • Drug use/abuse
  • Human reproduction
  • Identity/sexuality
  • Medical issues
  • Mental health & disorders
  • Social issues & ethics

Example Sensitive Questions

Examples in Biology

  • Would you have a child if you and your spouse are carriers for Huntington’s disease?
  • What do you think should be done with "extra" pre-embryos at a fertility clinic? Do they have a right to life? Who should get the embryos if the couple divorces or a partner dies?
  • Should efforts be made to save premature infants with a high chance of severe disabilities? What criteria should be used by doctors to decide which to let die?
  • How might racism among healthcare providers be recognized, monitored & countered?
  • In your opinion, is a doctor morally obligated to tell a patient their genetic testing results show they are nearly guaranteed to get Alzheimer's by age 80?
  • Where would you draw the line when deciding which animals (if any) are acceptable for use in research that might benefit humans?
  • Look back at our evolutionary line.What qualities would you say are necessary for a species to qualify as human?

Examples in Psychology

  • Should corporal punishment of children be banned?
  • If you were diagnosed with a terminal illness would you choose hospice care or a traditional death in a hospital? Why?
  • What are the potential ethical concerns associated with Milgram’s research on obedience?
  • If you had to choose to lose either your vision or your hearing, which would you choose and why?
  • What evidence exists for a genetic component to an individual’s IQ?
  • Which of the following elevates the risk for developing PTSD?
  • Describe several of the factors associated with suicide.

Examples in Business

  • Companies are not required to pay for employees' contraceptives for women if they (company owners) have religious objections, the Supreme Court ruled. Debate and discuss the effect this ruling has on businesses and society.
  • Is obesity considered a disability under the ADA? Do you think it should be?
  • How is Affirmative Action different from the Equal Opportunity Act?
  • Do you agree that companies should be allowed to claim that accommodating pregnant women is too expensive or less convenient and refuse to hire them? Why or why not?
  • In what circumstances can different employment standards for men and women be acceptable and not violate Title VII’s requirement for equality?
  • An employer placed an ad specifying applicants had to be US Citizens and present a Social Security card. Was this ad legal?
  • Describe a situation where an employee with a gun could make a workplace safer.
  • A company wants to collect personal employee data for a survey. How would you overcome the ethical considerations to collect, analyse and act on the data collected?
  • Employee development programs begin by identifying high potential candidates and offering them a chance to participate in the program. What are the consequences of this setting apart employees for “special treatment”?
  • Do you think companies should be penalized domestically based on how they do business abroad? Explain.
  • Do you believe that free trade agreements force consumers to trade the health and safety of their families for free trade? Explain.
  • What could be the career fallouts for a person who is unwilling or unable to train to become a better communicator?
  • How do you feel about the tracking of employees and the monitoring of your vital functions on the job and outside the workplace?
  • According to surveys, millennials voice the strongest opposition to government surveillance. If facing the choice between privacy and safety, which would you choose?

Authoring Solutions to Sensitive Questions

Solutions to questions involving sensitive topics should focus on facts and scientific evidence. Content in the solution should be aligned with the discussion of the topic in the textbook and informed by the author's expertise.

  • Solutions should include the objective information that a student needs to find the answer.
  • Solutions should never take a strong position, stance, or attitude on a sensitive topic
  • Narrative writing should be avoided.
  • Avoid opinion or prescriptive statements (e.g. "should")

If any information from outside sources is required to answer the question, the author must select and cite sources according to the Evelyn Learning Textbook Solutions Citation Guidelines.

Solutions that are sensitive and subjective should be authored using the ETBS Subjective Question Guidelines.

Reporting Inappropriate Solutions

If a solution in a review or QC assignment addresses a topic on the ETBS Sensitive Topics list, the solution should be carefully evaluated for appropriate treatment of the topic. Solutions that are inappropriate and may cause a reader to be offended or feel uncomfortable should be reported to the Team Lead responsible for the subject. The Team Lead will evaluate the solution and provide guidance for further action.

The reviewer can tag the Team Lead in a comment on the IH task containing the inappropriate question

There are several indicators that a solution to a sensitive question is inappropriate.

  • Inappropriate solutions can contain overgeneralizations, misleading statements, or false statements.
  • Inappropriate solutions can make unscientific or unsupported claims.
  • Inappropriate solutions can represent a specific opinion as "true".
