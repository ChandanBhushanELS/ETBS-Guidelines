Sample MCQ

Which of the following is not one of the five conditions of exchange?

A. each party will make an exchange with the other party.
B. each party potentially offers something of value to the other party.
C. each party is able to accept or reject the offer of the other party.
D. two parties must be present.

DOKDifficulty
2Medium

Solution

Answer

A

Explanation
  • A: An actual exchange is not one of the five conditions of exchange.
Incorrect Explanation(s)
  • B: For a potential exchange to take place, each party offers something valuable to the other party. Often, the consumer has money, while the producer has a product or service.
  • C: The producer can accept or reject the consumer's offer, and the consumer can accept or reject the producer's offer.
  • D: There will always be at least two parties: a producer and a consumer.
Hint(s)

Hint 1: The five conditions of exchange are preludes to a potential exchange.
Hint 2: An exchange can also be called a transaction.

Sample MS

Which of the following are categories of Porter's generic strategies? (Select all that apply.)

A. low-cost strategy
B. marketing strategy
C. upselling strategy
D. promotional strategy
E. focus strategy
F. differentiation strategy

DOKDifficulty
2Easy

Solution

Answer(s)

A

E

F

Explanation(s)
  • A: A low-cost strategy is one of Porter's generic strategies. It occurs when a brand's product aims for the lowest price in relation to competitors' prices.
  • E: A focus strategy is one of Porter's generic strategies. It occurs when a brand has a very specific target market.
  • F: A differentiation strategy is one Porter's generic strategies. It occurs when a product aims to be unique—generally in quality and durability—over its competitors.
Incorrect Explanation(s)
  • B: A marketing strategy is not included in Porter's generic strategies. A marketing strategy is a business's overall strategy to turn potential customers into customers.
  • C: An upselling strategy is not included in Porter's generic strategies. An upselling strategy is when a marketer persuades a consumer to spend more, upgrade, or purchase an additional good or service.
  • D: A promotional strategy is not included in Porter's generic strategies. A promotional strategy is the overall product communication strategy of a brand.
Hint(s)

Hint 1: Porter's generic strategies help a brand concentrate on its competitive advantage.
Hint 2: Porter's generic strategies are not mutually exclusive, meaning a brand can use more than one strategy.

Sample FIB

If the price of an elastic product is increased, the total revenue for the product will ________, whereas if the price of an inelastic product is increased, the total revenue for the product will ________.

DOKDifficulty
2Medium

Solution

Answer(s)
  • Decrease
  • Increase
Explanation(s)

A product is considered elastic when the percentage change in quantity demanded exceeds the percentage change in price; thus, an increase in price for an elastic product will generate an even greater decrease in quantity demanded for that good, decreasing revenue.


A product is considered inelastic when the percentage change in quantity demanded is less than the percentage change in price; thus, an increase in price for an inelastic product will generate a smaller decrease in quantity demanded for that good, increasing revenue.

Hint(s)

Hint 1: Consumers are more responsive to elastic goods. Elastic goods are often luxury goods and goods with many substitutes.
Hint 2: Consumers are less responsive to inelastic goods. Inelastic goods are often necessity goods and goods with few or no substitutes.

Sample SA


Describe the AIDA model and its four components.

DOKDifficulty
2Medium
Solution

The awareness, interest, desire, and action (AIDA) model is used in marketing to describe a consumer's buying process.
The stages of the buying process occur in the order listed:

  • Awareness is the stage in which a consumer is aware of a product.
  • Interest is the stage in which a consumer thinks about purchasing the product.
  • Desire is when a consumer has added a product to their in-store shopping cart or online shopping cart.
  • Action is the purchase or transaction.
Explanation(s)

The AIDA model is a step-by-step process used from when a consumer first becomes aware of a brand to the consumer making a purchase. Some consumers go through the AIDA model quickly when making a purchase; others take significant time going through the AIDA model.

Hint(s)

Hint: The AIDA model is often used in advertising or sales promotion.

Sample ES

Describe the four stages of the product life cycle, including sales and profits within each stage.

DOKDifficulty
2Medium

Solution

Sample Response

The four stages of the product life cycle are introduction, growth, maturity, and decline. A product life cycle illustrates the four stages on a chart, with sales on the Y axis and time on the X axis.

  • In the introduction stage, the product has been launched to the market for consumers to purchase. Costs associated with research and development are high in the introduction stage, making profits non-existent, while sales slowly increase.
  • In the growth stage, sales continue to increase and profits appear. It is the most important stage of the product life cycle, because it is when costs from the introduction stage are recouped. Also, the growth stage is the make-or-break stage, meaning if the product does not see significant growth, it may be pulled from the market.
  • The maturity stage is when a product sees the largest profits and the most sales. The maturity stage is when the product is at its highest popularity with consumers and is when a product peaks.
  • In the decline (i.e., final) stage, both sales and profits decrease due to a decrease in the consumers' demand for that product.
Explanation(s)

The product life cycle illustrates a product's complete journey from launch to decline and is often expressed as a diagram. Within the diagram, there are four stages: introduction, growth, maturity, and decline. The diagram features sales on the Y axis and time on the X axis. During the first three stages—introduction through maturity—sales are increasing. Once sales start to decrease, a product has reached its decline stage. Time is dependent upon consumer demand for the product.


Sample CALC

The price of a shirt is originally $36 with an in-store sales promotion of 20%. A customer purchases the shirt and uses a 10% off coupon. What is the final sales price?

DOKDifficulty
1Medium

Solution

Answer

$25.92

Explanation(s)
Step 1
Find the first discount price by subtracting the first discount amount from the original price.
$\begin{aligned} \text{First\:Discount\:Price}&= \text{Price} - \text{First\:Discount}\\&= \$36 - (\$36\times \:0.20)\\&= \$28.80 \end{aligned}%0$
Step 2
Find the second discount price by subtracting the second discount amount from the first discount price.
$\begin{aligned} \text{Second\:Discount\:Price}&= \text{First\:Discount\:Price} - \text{Second\:Discount}\\&= \$28.80 - (\$28.80\times \:.10)\\&= \$25.92 \end{aligned}%0$

Sample ORD

Order the stages of the new product development process.


  1. commercialization
  2. development
  3. idea screening
  4. business analysis
  5. new product strategy
  6. new product performance
  7. idea generation
  8. test marketing
DOKDifficulty
2Hard

Solution

Answer

The correct order is: E, G, C, D, B, H, A, F

Explanation(s)
  • The first stage is the new product strategy. This involves the realistic goals and strategies of a new product. Often, strengths and weaknesses of the overall business are assessed.
  • The second stage is idea generation. Idea generation involves using both internal sources, such as employees, and external sources, such as customer surveys, to try to find an ideal product. Often, there are many different ideas generated in this stage.
  • The third stage is idea screening. This stage filters the ideas generated from the previous stage and aims to narrow down the products that will likely be the most profitable.
  • The fourth stage is business analysis. In this stage, the business analyzes its current product mix and resource allocation. It takes into consideration things like current product mix, facilities, and capacity given its current resources. It also asks itself how the new product could change the overall business. It may change the overall business by number of employees, property, plant, equipment, or other capital expenses.
  • The fifth stage is development. Development occurs when the design, crafting, and production of the product takes place, prior to test marketing the product.
  • The sixth stage is test marketing. Test marketing occurs when a product is given to select consumers to try. Brands want to see their products as favorable to consumers, so the testing phase shows the overall product appeal to consumers.
  • The seventh step is commercialization. Once the brand knows its new product is favorable to consumers, based on the previous stage of test marketing, the new product is produced and launched in the commercialization stage. Commercialization is simply introducing a new product to the market.
  • The eighth stage is new product performance. Managers and marketers measure the product performance against its standard goals to see if the product outperformed or underperformed against its standards.
Hint(s)

Hint : This is a standard process—beginning with planning and ending with measurement—that businesses use to develop a tangible product.

Sample MAT

Match the following promotional mix elements with the examples below.

1. advertising
2. sales promotion
3. isocial media
4. personal selling
5. public relations

a. an in-store discount for a seasonal product
b. a television commercial airing during a sporting event
c. a car salesman negotiating with a potential customer
d. a nonprofit promoting a favorable image for a general audience
e. a digital promotion users of a social platform can see

DOKDifficulty
1Medium

Solution

Answer
  • b
  • a
  • e
  • c
  • d
Explanation(s)
  • b: Advertising is a paid-for, non-personal message. Television commercials are generally advertisements.
  • a: A sales promotion is an attempt to entice a consumer to buy a good, often with a discount-type tactic. An in-store discount for a seasonal product is a sales promotion.
  • e: Brands utilize digital promotions on social media for various reasons. Consumers likely have accounts on certain social media platforms and have the ability to see digital promotions.
  • c: Personal selling takes place when face-to-face interaction occurs between a salesperson and a consumer. A car salesman would use personal selling to best serve a consumer to make a purchase.
  • d: Public relations is used when an organization sends an unpaid message to a general audience, gaining exposure for a cause.
Hint(s)

Hint : The promotional mix includes a mix of marketing communication from a brand to a consumer.

Sample CL

Classify each of the following concepts as a 1) product, 2) price, 3) place, or 4) promotion.

a) psychological pricing strategies
b) Porter's generic strategies
c) distribution channels
d) product placement
e) slogans and jingles
f) break-even analysis


DOKDifficulty
2Medium

Solution

Answer
  • product: b)
  • price: a) f)
  • place: c)
  • promotion: d) e)
Explanation(s)
  • Porter's generic strategies are a product concept. If a brand has a specific generic strategy, it is designed for that purpose. The three main strategies are low-cost, focus, and differentiation. A low-cost strategy means a product wants to be less expensive than its competition. A focus strategy is when a product has a specific target market. A differentiation strategy is when a product is produced with unique features, such as being of better quality or more durable.
  • Psychological pricing strategies and break-even analysis fall under the price concept. Psychological pricing strategies are marketing tactics that strive to make prices look more attractive to a consumer. There are various forms of psychological pricing strategies that marketers use.
    Break-even analysis is a cost-based calculation that aims to project the number of units of a product that must be sold to cover its own fixed and variable costs.
  • Distribution channels fall under the place concept. Distribution channels, or marketing channels, are the path a product takes to get from producer to consumer. There are four typical distribution channels and the channel is dependent upon the product.
  • Product placement and slogan and jingles fall under the promotion concept. Product placement is a form of advertising and is often found in television shows and movies. Product placement is when an individual talks about a name brand product or is seen using or consumer a name brand within a production.
    Slogans and jingles are short and memorable marketing tactics. Slogans are generally phrases, whereas jingles are slogans with a tune or music. Some brands aim to achieve brand recognition with their slogans and jingles.

Hint(s)

Hint: These four make up the marketing mix, which aims to reach its marketing objectives and fulfill the needs of its target market.
