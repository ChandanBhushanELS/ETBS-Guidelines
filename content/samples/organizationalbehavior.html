Sample MCQ

Sample ES

State the three ways a person or organization can create dependence. Explain how they work and provide one example for each.

DOKDifficulty
2Medium

Solution

Sample Response

There are three ways a person or an organization can create dependence:

  • Achieve importance. For example, an individual will have importance if they are the key knowledge holder of a critical information; an organization creates importance through products or services that are of key value to stakeholders.
  • Possess a scarce resource. For example, an engineer who created the main code for a software company has scarce knowledge about the composition of the code architecture. A company creates scarcity when it offers a product or service that is very difficult to find.
  • Create non-substitutability. For example, a person can create such dependence by patenting an invention, while an organization creates it by introducing an invention to the market.
Explanation(s)

To create dependence, the resource the firm has should be characterized as important, scarce, or non substitutable, orâ€”best of allâ€”should have all three characteristics.

  • The first way to create dependence is through importance. When a firm offers a resource/product fulfilling an important need, people will come searching for it. For example, consider Company T entering the automobile market and offering a fully electric vehicle which provided an important green substitute for the fossil-fuel-driven cars that were saturating the market.
  • The second way to create dependence is by controlling a resource that is scarce. When the supply of such a resource is not abundant, there is a competition over who can get it, which makes people dependent on the owner of that resource. Continuing with the earlier example, Company T created a product that was scarce because there were few competitors in the electric vehicle market, and none offered the advanced featuresâ€”such as extended rangeâ€”of the Company T cars.
  • The third way to create dependency is through a product that is not substitutable or doesnâ€™t have competitors. In such cases, the resource owner makes others dependent on them because there is not an alternative to the ownerâ€™s resources. In the above case, Company T used the non substitutability principle by adding features to its cars that were not available in any other electric vehicle, such as long milage, luxury, and a self-driving feature.

    Order the steps in the rational decision-making model from first to last.


    1. develop alternatives
    2. identify the decision criteria
    3. select the best alternative
    4. define the problem
    5. allocate weights to the criteria
    6. evaluate the alternatives

    DOKDifficulty
    2Easy

    Solution

    Answer

    The correct order is: 4, 2, 5, 1, 6, 3

    Explanation
    • The first step in decision making is to identify the problem at hand.
    • Next, the decision-maker needs to identify all the criteria that are relevant to the problem and will affect the rationality of the decision made, e.g. price, resources needed, efficiency, etc.
    • Once a list of criteria is generated, the next logical step is to assign weights to each criterion. For example, the decision-maker should decide on two criteria: e.g., price and efficiency. Is low price as important as efficiency? If yes, assign those criteria equal weightsâ€”50% each; if price is three times more important than efficiency, assign 75% weight to price and 25% weight to efficiency.
    • After the decision-making criteria are settled, the decision maker needs to develop alternative solutions to the problem.
    • The next step is to evaluate each of the alternative solutions based on the criteria decided upon under steps 2 and 3.
    • Finally, the decision maker needs to choose the best alternative according to the evaluations in step 5.
    Hint

    Hint 1: The rational decision-making model is built on consequent logical steps, such that each preceding step should be completed before the next one is attempted.
    Hint 2: The order of the steps should make logical sense.

    2Medium

    Solution

    Answer
    • d
    • c
    • a
    • e
    • b
    Explanation
    • d: When competing in a conflict, the goal of each party is to pursue their own needs or goals because those goals are of the utmost importance to the parties. This could come at a high price, such as hurting the relationship with the opposing party or building a negative image.
    • c: When collaborating, the parties in a conflict are working together with the goal of achieving a solution that is at least somewhat satisfying to all involved.
    • a: Avoiding is a strategy of staying away from a conflict because it is of little concernâ€”or without any expected benefitsâ€”to the uninvolved party.
