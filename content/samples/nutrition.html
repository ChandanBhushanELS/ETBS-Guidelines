Sample MCQ

Which statement about essential nutrients is correct?

A. There are three classes of essential nutrients.
B. They can be produced in adequate quantities by the body.
C. They are not necessary requirements of the diet.
D. Both essential nutrients and non-essential nutrients are required by the body.

DOKDifficulty
1Easy

Solution

Answer

D

The answer block should contain only the answer identifier (A, B, C, D, etc.) formatted in the same way as the question (lowercase vs. uppercase).
Explanation
  • D: Essential nutrients such as proteins, carbohydrates, and lipids provide energy, as well as the building blocks for body tissues. Non-essential nutrients can be produced by the body. Cholesterol is a non-essential nutrient involved in many processes, including the production of bile (emulsifies lipids).
Incorrect Explanation(s)
  • A: There are six classes of essential nutrients: minerals, water, lipids, proteins, carbohydrates, and vitamins.
  • B: Essential nutrients are not produced by the body, so they must be obtained from sources outside the body.
  • C: The essential part of the name essential nutrients refers to the fact external sources must provide these nutrients. The body takes in these essential nutrients through food.
Hint(s)

Hint: Essential nutrients include macronutrients and micronutrients.

Sample TCQ (TF)

True or false: All energy-yielding nutrients go through the tricarboxylic acid cycle as the final metabolic pathway step of extracting energy.

DOKDifficulty
1Easy

Solution

Answer

True

Explanation
  • Through hydrolysis, fats break down into fatty acids and glycerol. Fatty acids further break down with the help of Î²-oxidation into multiple two-carbon fragments that, together with the coenzyme CoA, form acetyl CoA. Acetyl CoA molecules can enter the tricarboxylic acid (TCA) cycle.
  • Glucose from carbohydrates breaks down to become pyruvate with the help of coenzymes. When the coenzyme CoA is added to pyruvate, a multi-step process results in the formation of acetyl CoA molecules that then enter the TCA cycle. One glucose molecule produces two pyruvate molecules in this pathway, and the two pyruvate molecules lead to the production of two acetyl CoA molecules.
  • Proteins break down into amino acids. Some amino acids can become pyruvate, which can enter the TCA cycle. Other amino acids become acetyl CoA directly (through oxidative degradation), while other amino acids directly enter the TCA cycle as glucogenic amino acids. Different amino acids enter the TCA cycle at different points, then complete the remainder of the cycle.
Hint

Hint: The tricarboxylic acid (TCA) cycle takes place within the inner compartment of mitochondria.

Sample SA

Contrast the solubility, transport, and storage of vitamin A and the B vitamins.

DOKDifficulty
2Medium

Solution

Sample Response
  • Vitamin A is a fat-soluble vitamin. Fat soluble vitamins are hydrophobic. These vitamins enter the lymphatic system, then the blood, where they typically bind to a protein.The liver and fatty tissues store the vitamins until they are needed in the body.
  • The eight B vitamins are hydrophilic (i.e., water-soluble). Hydrophilic vitamins are able to travel into blood directly, and they travel without binding to a protein. Water-soluble vitamins can also move freely in aqueous compartments of the bodyâ€™s cells. The body does not store excess B vitamins. Excess water-soluble vitamins are excreted from the body in the urine.
Explanation
  • Vitamin A is a fat-soluble vitamin, along with vitamins D, E, and K. Fat-soluble vitamins are normally present in oils and fats of food. These vitamins are more likely to be stored in the liver and adipose tissues without being removed from the body via excretion.
  • The eight B vitamins and vitamin C are water-soluble. These vitamins are typically present in the water content in food. Excesses are removed from the body via the kidneys, so they have to be ingested more often than fat-soluble vitamins.

Sample ES

Your friend was recently diagnosed with type 2 diabetes. He states that his goals include a desire to improve his glucose control and other eating habits. He mentions hearing about a diet that incorporates the glycemic index in meal planning. He asks if you consider the diet to be advantageous and practical. What is your response?

DOKDifficulty
2Medium

Solution

Sample Response

The term glycemic response refers to how quickly glucose is absorbed following a meal, how high blood glucose increases, and how soon blood glucose goes back to normal range.

A low glycemic response to a meal is ideal for good health. A low response consists of slow absorption, a moderate increase in blood glucose, and a smooth descent back to normal. In contrast, rapid absorption, a steep increase in blood glucose, and a drastic drop in blood glucose below normal range are not ideal for good health. These are characteristics of a high glycemic response to a meal.

Regulating glycemic responses is especially important to individuals with diabetes mellitus, who can benefit from restricting the intake of foods that cause too sudden an increaseâ€”or decreaseâ€”in blood glucose.

Different foods evoke specific and predictable glycemic responses. Foods are assigned a glycemic index value based on the glycemic responses they produce. Some research has shown that choosing foods with a low glycemic index is a beneficial way to improve glucose control and dietary adequacy.

Researching the glycemic index values for every food is not required for a glycemic index-based diet. Medical organizations have suggestions for diets with both low and moderate glycemic index options. These types of diets support control of cholesterol and weight loss. It is useful to encourage individuals with diabetes mellitus to eat small meals more often in order to evenly stretch glucose absorption throughout the day. This practice offers more advantages compared to a diet based on foods with a low glycemic response. Individuals who desire a low-glycemic diet should avoid following a low-carbohydrate diet concurrently. A diet that is both low-glycemic and low-carbohydrate likely would fail to provide adequate nutrients, as well as the energy necessary for daily activities.

Explanation

Individuals with type 2 diabetes mellitus have difficulty regulating blood glucose levels. Cells have difficulty taking up blood glucose, so blood glucose levels remain high. The pancreas tries to compensate by secreting more insulin. The pancreas eventually becomes less able to compensate, and the hyperglycemia (high blood glucose) becomes worse. Due to the bodyâ€™s difficulty regulating blood glucose, individuals with type 2 diabetes mellitus must always be mindful of their blood glucose levels and make wise diet choices.

Low to moderate glycemic index foods includeâ€”but are not limited toâ€”fruits, legumes, vegetables, and milk. A diet high in these foods is generally encouraged for most people. A diabetes-specific recommendation includes eating more frequent small meals.

Sample CALC

Calculate the energy available from a bagel with cream cheese that contains 8 g of fat, 66 g carbohydrates, and 14 g protein.

DOKDifficulty
2Medium

Solution

Answer

392 kcal

The answer block should contain the final answer to the calculation (inclusive of units, if applicable). Then provide the step-by-step work that was used to arrive at the answer in the step_by_step block. This should be broken down into logical steps that the student will be able to understand even if they struggle to understand the entire problem.
Explanation
Step 1
Fat contains approximately 9 kcal/g. Carbohydrates and proteins both contain approximately 4 kcal/g. Based on this information, the kcal/g for the entire meal can be calculated using the amount of energy in each nutrient in the food.
First, we must calculate the amount of energy provided by each nutrient type.
$$\begin{aligned}\mathrm{(8\;g)\times\left(9\;\frac{kcal}{g}\right)}&=\mathrm{72\;kcal\;from\;fat}\\\mathrm{(66\;g)\times\left(4\;\frac{kcal}{g}\right)}&=\mathrm{264\;kcal\;from\;carbohydrates}\\\mathrm{(14\;g)\times\left(4\;\frac{kcal}{g}\right)}&=\mathrm{56\;kcal\;from\;protein}\end{aligned}$$
Step 2
Energy is provided by proteins, fats, and carbohydrates. The amount of kcal provided by each nutrient can be added together to provide the available energy from the entire meal.
$$\mathrm{72\;kcal+264\;kcal+56\;kcal=392\;kcal\;available}$$

Sample ORD

Arrange the steps of lipid digestion in order from beginning to end:

a) bile performs emulsification
b) lingual lipase performs hydrolysis
c) gastric lipase hydrolyzes fat
d) pancreatic lipase creates monoglycerides

DOKDifficulty
2Medium

Solution

Answer

The correct order is: b, c, a, d

Explanation
  • b: Lingual lipase hydrolyzes fats in the mouth. Lingual lipase is a secretion from the sublingual salivary gland.
  • c: Lipids interact with the gastric lipase enzyme in the stomach. Gastric lipase is made by chief cells located in gastric glands of the stomach. This enzyme hydrolyzes triglycerides.
  • a: Bile emulsifies fat, breaking it into smaller droplets. This process occurs in the small intestine, where bile enters the duodenum via the hepatopancreatic ampulla. Bile is produced by the liver and stored in the gallbladder. The hormone cholecystokinin (CCK) stimulates release of bile from the gallbladder.
  • d: Pancreatic lipase breaks fat droplets into fatty acids and glycerol. Pancreatic lipase is contained in pancreatic juice, which is secreted by the pancreas.
Hint(s)

Hint: Fatty acids and glycerol are the monomers of lipids.

Sample DRW

Describe and draw the structure of two fatty acids, stearic acid and linoleic acid, including the number of carbons and type of bonds.

DOKDifficulty
2Medium

Solution

Answer
Provide a sample graphic (art asset) and then provide a description of the graphic immediately following the asset. See the Images section for specific instructions on how to generate art assets for solutions. Follow this description with clear instructions directing students how to develop, label, and format their drawings to satisfy the question.

Stearic acid is a saturated fatty acid with a fatty acid chain length of 18 carbons.

  • The carbons which make up the backbone of the fatty acid chain are all connected by single bonds.
  • Saturated fatty acids, such as stearic acid, have straight carbon chains connected entirely by single bonds with hydrogen atoms bonded to the carbon chain at every possible position.

Linoleic acid is an unsaturated fatty acid with a fatty acid chain length of 18 carbons.

  • The carbons which make up the backbone of the fatty acid chain are connected by mostly single bonds, but also two double bonds (between the sixth and seventh carbon and the ninth and 10th carbon). Having more than one double bond makes linoleic acid a polyunsaturated acid.
  • Unsaturated fatty acids, such as linoleic acid, have at least one double bond in the carbon chain. These double bonds mark where there is an empty position without a hydrogen atom bonded to the carbon chain. The fatty acid chain bends at the location of these double bonds. Linoleic acid has two double bonds, so the fatty acid has two bends.

Explanation

Every fatty acid has the same general structure: a chain of carbon atoms with hydrogens bonded to them, along with a methyl group (CH3) at one end and an acid group (COOH) located at the other end. Fatty acids vary in their carbon chain length, as well as the location and number of their double bonds.

Most fatty acids that occur naturally have even numbers of carbons within their chains. Long-chain fatty acids contain more than 12 carbons. These are commonly found in meats, vegetable oils, and seafood. Medium-chain (containing between eight and 12 carbon atoms) and short-chain (containing a maximum of six carbons) fatty acids are mainly found in dairy products.

Sample GR

Create a line graph that shows the BMI of a 6 ft tall individual at following body weights, in pounds: 132, 154, 184, 228.

DOKDifficulty
2Medium

Solution

Answer
Explanation
Step 1
Body mass index (BMI) is a calculation that takes into account a person's height and weight. The formula for BMI is $\mathrm{BMI}=\frac{m}{h^2}$ where $m$ is a person's mass in kilograms and $h$ is their height in meters.

Before using the BMI formula, weight needs to be converted from pounds to kilograms (1 lb is equal to 0.45 kg) and height needs to be converted from feet to meters (1 ft = 0.3048 m).
$$\begin{aligned}h=\mathrm{(6\;ft)\times\left(0.3048\;\frac{m}{ft}\right)}&=\mathrm{1.828\;m}\\m_1=\mathrm{(132\;lb)\times\left(.45\;\frac{kg}{lb}\right)}&=\mathrm{59.4\;kg}\\m_2=\mathrm{(154\;lb)\times\left(.45\;\frac{kg}{lb}\right)}&=\mathrm{69.3\;kg}\\m_3=\mathrm{(184\;lb)\times\left(.45\;\frac{kg}{lb}\right)}&=\mathrm{82.8\;kg}\\m_4=\mathrm{(228\;lb)\times\left(.45\;\frac{kg}{lb}\right)}&=\mathrm{102.6\;kg}\end{aligned}$$
Step 2
Use the BMI formula for each weight (in kilograms).$$\mathrm{BMI}=\frac{m}{h^2}$$
$$\begin{aligned}\mathrm{BMI_1=\frac{59.4\;kg}{(1.828)^2}}&=\mathrm{17.78\;kg/m^2}\\\mathrm{BMI_2=\frac{69.3\;kg}{(1.828)^2}}&=\mathrm{20.74\;kg/m^2}\\\mathrm{BMI_3=\frac{82.8\;kg}{(1.828)^2}}&=\mathrm{24.78\;kg/m^2}\\\mathrm{BMI_4=\frac{102.6\;kg}{(1.828)^2}}&=\mathrm{30.7\;kg/m^2}\end{aligned}$$

Sample CL

Classify the following as foods that contain mostly saturated fats or unsaturated fats.

a) whole milk
b) canola oil
c) sunflower seeds
d) cheese
e) pistachios
f) eggs
g) sour cream


DOKDifficulty
1Easy

Solution

Answer
  • S.F: a, d, g
  • U.F: b, c, e, f
Explanation(s)
  • S.F: Whole milk and milk products contain saturated fats. Saturated fats often are solid or viscous at room temperature due to the chemical structure of the saturated fatty acid chains. More hydrogen bonds make fatty acid chains straighter, and straight chains can pack together closely. Saturated fats can be part of a healthy diet, but overconsumption of saturated fats has been linked to health issues such as heart disease.
  • U.F: Plant products contain unsaturated fats. Unsaturated fats are found mainly within plants and fish. These fats are often liquid at room temperature due to the chemical structure of the unsaturated fatty acid chains. Double bonds in the fatty acid chain make the chain bend and reduce the ability of the chains to pack together closely. Unsaturated fats are an important part of a healthy diet. Unsaturated fats have been linked to decreases in blood cholesterol, which can block blood vessels.

Hint: Nuts typically fall under the category of healthful lipids.
