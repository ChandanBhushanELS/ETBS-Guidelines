Sample MCQ

A manager is conducting a SWOT analysis of its most popular brand. What phase of management is this?
A. planning
B. organizing
C. leading
D. controlling

DOKDifficulty
1Easy

Solution

Answer

A

Explanation
  • A: A SWOT analysis takes place in the planning phase of management, as it is a form of research to help the company set goals.
Incorrect Explanation(s)
  • B: The organizing phase of management consists of action steps toward the achievement of goals, whereas a SWOT analysis is a form of research to determine the action steps to take.
  • C: Leading pertains to the development and encouragement of others toward the achievement of their own professional goals as well as those of the organization. While leading is perpetually occurring throughout all phases of management, this is not the phase in which a SWOT analysis is typically conducted.
  • D: Controlling consists of monitoring, benchmarking, and making improvements to processes and procedures based on actual outcomes compared to previously set goals. A SWOT analysis does not occur in this stage.
Hint(s)

Hint 1: A SWOT analysis focuses on understanding an organization’s strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats.
Hint 2: A company wanting to restructure would need to begin with a thorough SWOT analysis.

Sample MS

Which of the following are included in Porter’s five forces? (Select all that apply.) A. threat of new consumers
B. bargaining power of suppliers
C. bargaining power of competitors
D. threats of substitute products or services

DOKDifficulty
1Easy

Solution

Answer(s)

B

D

Explanation
  • B: Suppliers affect the competitive environment by raising prices or limiting the availability of products and services. They are one of Porter’s five forces.
  • D: Threats of substitute goods and services are one of Porter’s five forces. The competitiveness of an organization can be compromised if rivals offer a similar product or service at a lower price.
Incorrect Explanation(s)
  • A: Consumers are not threats as defined by Porter’s five forces. Instead, they provide opportunities to increase an organization’s market share.
  • C: Competitors do not have bargaining power with a competing organization, as they are not part of the same value chain. They are not one of Porter’s five forces.
Hint

A force shapes the competitive environment of an organization or industry.

Sample ES

State the four principal functions of a manager, explain what they are, and provide one reason as to why they’re important.

DOKDifficulty
2Medium

Solution

Explanation

There are four major functions of a manager’s role: planning, organizing, controlling, and leading. At the onset of a managerial role, the functions of planning, organizing, leading, and controlling may happen in sequence. However, any of the functions may be revisited at any time as necessary.

Planning starts with defining the organization’s mission, vision, and values. Then, it involves determining both short- and long-term objectives and developing strategies to achieve the organization’s mission and vision. This function of management is important because it provides a blueprint to follow so everyone in the organization is on the same page and can work cohesively toward the same goal.

Organizing is the function that focuses on assigning tasks to achieve the objectives and goals of the organization. This includes the assignment of the best people to accomplish these goals. Organizing is important because it focuses on the creation of an action plan for the goals and objectives developed in the planning phase.

Leading involves motivating and influencing employees to work hard and keeping them engaged. The management function of leading is one that should be occurring at every point in time. It includes encouraging employees to fulfill their own professional goals as well as helping others to see their purpose in the organization as a whole.

Controlling involves monitoring processes closely, benchmarking against standards, and taking corrective actions when necessary. It is a means of creating accountability toward achieving goals in a timely manner and assures that activities are being executed as planned.

Sample CALC

Company Q has the following data for the end of the year:

  • revenues: $5,000,000
  • expenses: $3,000,000
What is the net profit (loss) margin?

DOKDifficulty
1Easy

Solution

Answer

40%

Explanation
Step 1
Calculate net income or net loss.
$\begin{aligned}\text{Net Income}&= \text{Revenues − Expenses}\\&={\$5,000,000}−{\$3,000,000}\\&={\$2,000,000}\end{aligned}$
Step 2
Calculate the net profit (loss) margin.
$\begin{aligned}\text{Net Profit Margin}&=\dfrac{\text{Net Income}}{\text{Revenues}}\\& =\dfrac{\$2,000,000}{\$5,000,000} \\& = 40\% \end{aligned}$

Sample ORD

Order the five levels of Maslow’s hierarchy of needs from the most basic to the highest level.

self-actualization
physiological
esteem
belonging
safety

DOKDifficulty
2Easy

Solution

Answer

The correct order is: 5, 1, 4, 3, 2

Explanation
  • In order to attain any level of achievement, an individual must first have their basic human needs met, e.g., food and water. Such needs comprise the first level of Maslow’s hierarchy of needs.
  • Once an individual’s basic physiological needs are met, the second level of Maslow’s hierarchy of needs focuses on the need for shelter, also known as safety.
  • Once basic human needs and safety are both met in an individual’s life, they can focus on the third level of Maslow’s hierarchy of needs: belonging or love.
  • Once basic human needs, safety, and belonging are all met in an individual’s life, they can focus on the fourth level of Maslow’s Hierarchy of Needs: esteem.
  • Finally, once basic human needs, safety, belonging, and esteem are met in an individual’s life, they can focus on the fifth level of Maslow’s hierarchy of needs: self-actualization.
Hint

Maslow’s hierarchy of needs are achieved from the bottom to the top of the pyramid.

Sample CL

Classify the following as current assets, fixed assets, or liabilities:

  • buildings
  • accounts payable
  • accounts receivable
  • interest payable
  • equipment
  • inventory

DOKDifficulty
2Easy

Solution

Answer
  • Current assets: inventory, accounts receivable
  • Fixed assets: buildings, equipment
  • Liabilities: accounts payable, interest payable
Explanation
  • Current assets are those that can be converted to cash within one year. Inventory and accounts receivable are both assets that can be converted to cash quickly.
  • Fixed assets have a longer useful life and usually take more than one year to convert to cash. Buildings and equipment generally last longer than one year and are not sold as quickly as current assets.
  • Liabilities are forms of debt and represent money owed. Accounts payable and interest payable are both liability accounts.
Hint(s)

Hint 1: The quicker an asset can be sold for cash, the more current of an asset it is.

Hint 2: Fixed assets tend to be purchased much less frequently than current assets.

Hint 3: Accounts that classify as liabilities tend to have the same terminology within the account titles.

Sample SA (Operations Management)

What is the difference between a Green Belt and a Black Belt in Six Sigma projects?

DOKDifficulty
1Easy

Solution

Answer

An individual with a Green Belt level of training in Six Sigma will help to train others in the process part-time while performing normal duties the rest of the time. An individual with a Black Belt level of training will take on the full-time lead of the project.

Explanation
  • There are multiple levels of Six Sigma certification. The Black Belt is a mastery level of training, and individuals trained at this level will take the project lead. Those trained at the Green Belt level will be able to start taking on smaller training roles within the project while being mentored by someone with more training.
Hint

As an individual attains higher Six Sigma training levels, the responsibilities become more focused on the project at large rather than individual tasks at the operational level.

Sample CALC (Operations Management)

A museum’s gift shop needs to maintain enough inventory of its most popular coffee mug. Annual demand for the mug is 8,500 units, and the lead time from the manufacturer is 3 weeks.
What is the reorder point?

DOKDifficulty
2Medium

Solution

Answer

491 mugs

Explanation
Step 1
Calculate the weekly demand of coffee mugs by dividing the annual demand by 52 weeks.
$\begin{aligned}\text{Weekly Demand}&=\dfrac{\text{Annual Demand}}{\text{52 Weeks}}\\&=\dfrac{8{,}500}{52}\\&=163{.}5\end{aligned}$
Step 2
Calculate the reorder point by multiplying the weekly demand of mugs by the number of weeks of lead time from the manufacturer. Round up to the nearest whole number.
$\begin{aligned}\text{Reorder Point}&=\text{Weekly Demand}\times \text{Lead Time from Manufacturer}\\&=163{.}5 \times3\\&=490{.}5\\&=491\end{aligned}$

Sample DRW (Operations Management)

On January 1, 2020, a large suburban bakery decided to enter the regional bridal show to be held on March 1, 2020, in order to try to increase sales in the next year. Based on the tasks and the start and end dates of each activity, create a Gantt chart, then comment on anything of particular importance that may surface and make suggestions based on the chart.

Start Date End Date
Project Bridal Show 2020-01-01 2020-03-01
Develop marketing materials 2020-01-01 2020-02-10
Photograph samples 2020-01-10 2020-01-20
Assemble goodie bags 2020-02-01 2020-02-28
Prepare food samples 2020-02-25 2020-02-28
DOKDifficulty
3Hard

Solution

Answer

Explanation

The Gantt chart of the project reveals several important items of interest:

  • There is a small window of time to photograph and gather samples of the products, and there appears to be a week and a half between the photograph end date and the marketing materials end date.
    • Suggestion: request a small extension on the photograph activity to February 1.
  • Preparing the food samples must occur at the end of the project and is highly time sensitive.
    • Suggestion: move the end date of assembling the goodie bags up to February 20.
    • Suggestion: move the start date of preparing the food samples to February 22 and assign preparation tasks.
