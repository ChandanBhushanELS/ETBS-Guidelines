Answer

True

Explanation

The term eupnea is derived from the prefix eu-, meaning normal, and the suffix -pnea, meaning breathing. Eupnea is characterized by breathing that is quiet and unlabored. Any deviations from this, such as excessive gasping, are abnormal.

Sample ES

Explain the clinical importance of universally accepted directional and anatomical terms.

DOKDifficulty
2Medium

Solution

Sample Response

Within the medical community, sophisticated terminology has been developed for use in clinical settings. Such terminology is used to:

  • define individual regions of the body
  • describe the positions of body parts in relation to one another
  • divide the body according to anatomical planes
  • define body positioning
  • divide the abdominopelvic regions

Universally accepted medical language ensures accurate and efficient communication among clinicians from around the world. The language provides all medical professions the ability to document and interpret medical records, patient testimonials, test results, etc. Without proper terminology, there could be misunderstandings that lead to errors and gaps or delays in diagnosis and subsequent treatment.

For example, paramedics are the first responders who assess the injuries of patients involved in automobile accidents. In these instances, paramedics must examine individuals for internal injuries. The paramedic must be able to quickly and accurately describe the locations of these injuries to the emergency room clinicians so they know where to focus their treatment. Using directional and anatomical terms allows for quick and accurate communication.

Explanation

Medical terminology specifies region, direction, depth, distance, position, and relationship between structures.

  • Individual regions of the body are given unique terms. For example, the upper arm is known as the brachial region. Structures associated with this region include the brachial nerve and biceps brachii muscle.
  • Directional terms are used to describe the location of one body part in relation to another. For instance, the term superior is used to describe body parts that are above another body part. For example, the eyes are superior to the nose.
  • Anatomical planes are imaginary surfaces that can be used to divide the body into two segments. These terms are often used in a radiological setting to properly take an image of an organ. For example, a transverse section divides the body into a superior and inferior portion. This is often used to visualize individual abdominopelvic organs.
  • Body positioning terms are used to define how a patient should be orientated during an exam or procedure. For example, the lithotomy position is described as an individual placed horizontally on their back, with both their legs and knees bent and their legs rotated outward. This is often used for gynecological exams.
  • Abdominopelvic regions spatially divide the abdomen in order to more precisely identify organ dysfunction(s). For example, an individual experiencing pain in the upper left quadrant of their abdomen may have an issue with their stomach.

Sample CALC

A patient has 10 mL of blood drawn. Of this, 6 mL is composed of plasma, and the buffy coat makes up 0.2 mL. What is the patientâ€™s hematocrit?

DOKDifficulty
2Medium

Solution

Answer

38%

The answer block should contain the final answer to the calculation (inclusive of units, if applicable). Then provide the step-by-step work that was used to arrive at the answer in the step_by_step block. This should be broken down into logical steps that the student will be able to understand even if they struggle to understand the entire problem.
Explanation
Step 1
Hematocrit is defined as the percentage of blood that is composed of red blood cells (RBCs). Two equations are needed to determine hematocrit:
$$\begin{aligned}\mathrm{Volume\;of\;RBCs}&=\mathrm{Volume\;of\;blood-plasma\;volume-\;volume\;of\;buffy\;coat}\\\mathrm{Hematocrit}&=\mathrm{(Volume\;of\;RBCs/volume\;of\;blood)\times100\%}\end{aligned}$$
Step 2
First, determine the volume of RBCs.
$$\begin{aligned}\mathrm{Volume\;of\;RBCs}&=\mathrm{10\;mL-6\;mL-0.2\;mL}\\\mathrm{Volume\;of\;RBCs}&=\mathrm{3.8\;mL}\end{aligned}$$
Step 3
Next, use the volume of RBCs to determine the hematocrit.
$$\begin{aligned}\mathrm{Hematocrit}&=\mathrm{(3.8\;mL/10\;mL)\times100\%}\\\mathrm{Hematocrit}&=38\%\end{aligned}$$

Sample ORD

Arrange the combining forms of the corresponding gastrointestinal structures in order from the beginning of the gastrointestinal tract to the end.

a) proct/o
b) an/o
c) gastr/o
d) or/o
e) enter/o
f) esophag/o
g) col/o

DOKDifficulty
2Medium

Solution

Answer

The correct order is: d, f, c, e, g, a, b

Explanation
  • d: The combining form or/o means pertaining to the mouth. For example, the oral cavity refers to the mucous membrane-lined space within the mouth.
  • f: The combining form esophag/o means pertaining to the esophagus. For example, an esophagogram is a radiological image that is used to assess the swallowing function of the esophagus.
  • c: The combining form gastr/o means pertaining to the stomach. For example, gastritis refers to inflammation of the stomach.
  • e: The combining form enter/o means pertaining to the small intestine. For example, enteropathy refers to any particular disease of the small intestine.
  • g: The combining form col/o means pertaining to the large intestine. For example, a colonoscopy is an exam that uses a flexible tube and camera to visualize structures of the large intestine.
  • a: The combining form proct/o means pertaining to the rectum. For example, proctology is a field of medicine that focuses on disorders of the rectum.
  • b: The combining form an/o means pertaining to the anus. For example, the anal sphincter is a muscular anatomical structure that controls the passage of stool.
Hint(s)

Hint: The gastrointestinal tract functions to digest and absorb nutrients.

Sample DRW

Draw the human body in anatomical position. Include labeled arrows corresponding to the major directional terms.

DOKDifficulty
2Medium

Solution

Answer

See the Images section for specific instructions on how to generate art assets for solutions. Follow this description with clear instructions directing students how to develop, label, and format their drawings to satisfy the question.


Explanation

A universal anatomical position is used when describing the relative location of body parts. This anatomical position is defined as an individual with:

  • an erect position
  • feet shoulder-width apart
  • eyes facing forward
  • arms at the side
  • palms facing forward

Directional terms are used to describe the locations of one body part in relation to another. Use of such terms ensures consistency among medical professionals when describing such positioning. The major directional terms include:

  • Superior (means above): For example, the eyes are superior to the nose.
  • Inferior (means below): For example, the ankles are inferior to the knees.
  • Medial (means toward the middle): For example, the nose is medial to the ears.
  • Lateral (means toward the outer portions of the body): For example, the thumb is lateral to the little finger.
  • Anterior (means in front): For example, the chest is anterior to the back.
  • Posterior (means behind): For example, the esophagus is posterior to the trachea.
  • Proximal (means toward the point of attachment): For example, the thigh is proximal to the knee.
  • Distal (means away from the point of attachment): For example, the wrist is distal to the elbow.

Sample CL

Classify each term pertaining to the ear as a 1) disorder, 2) diagnostic tool, or 3) procedure.

a) tympanoplasty
b) otoscope
c) otorrhea
d) audiometer
e) mastoiditis
f) stapedectomy


DOKDifficulty
1Easy

Solution

Answer
  • 1: c, e
  • 2: b, d
  • 3: a, f
Explanation(s)
  • 1: Disorders of the ear include otorrhea and mastoiditis.
    • Otorrhea is a condition marked by excessive ear discharge. The term is derived from the combining form ot/o, meaning ear, and the suffix -rrhea, meaning discharge.
    • Mastoiditis is a condition marked by inflammation of the mastoid process. The term is derived from the combining form mastoid/o, meaning mastoid process, and the suffix -itis, meaning inflammation.
  • 2: Diagnostic tools used to assess ear structure and function include an otoscope and audiometer.
    • An otoscope is a tool used to examine structures within the ear. The term is derived from the combining form ot/o, meaning ear, and the suffix -scope, meaning a tool used to visually examine a structure.
    • An audiometer is a tool used to assess the hearing function of an individual. The term is derived from the combining form audi/o, meaning hearing, and the suffix meter, meaning a tool that can perform measurements.
  • 3: Procedures performed on the ear include tympanoplasty and stapedectomy.
    • A tympanoplasty refers to a surgical procedure in which the middle ear is repaired. The term is derived from the combining form tympan/o, meaning middle ear, and the suffix -plasty, meaning molding or repair.
    • A stapedectomy refers to a surgical procedure in which the stapes is removed. The term is derived from the combining form staped/o, meaning the stapes, and the suffix -ectomy, meaning surgical removal.

Hint(s)

Hint: The ear is divided into the outer ear, middle ear, and inner ear regions.
