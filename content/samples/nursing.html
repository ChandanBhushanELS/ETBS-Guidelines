Sample MCQ

During a patient assessment which finding would require an intervention?

a. skin color of the chest is similar to that of the face
b. breathing at a rate of 15 breaths per minute
c. a 45 degree downward slope of the ribs
d. the anteroposterior diameter of the chest is greater than that of the transverse diameter

DOKDifficulty
2Medium

Solution

Answer

d

The answer block should contain only the answer identifier (A, B, C, D, etc.) formatted in the same way as the question (lowercase vs. uppercase).
Explanation
  • d: The transverse diameter (measured from the left lateral edge to the right lateral edge) of the chest is normally larger than the anteroposterior diameter (measured from the anterior surface to the posterior surface) of the chest. The anteroposterior diameter increases in conditions such as emphysema, during which individuals have difficulty expiring air from the lungs. This leads to chronic hyperinflation of the lungs.
Incorrect Explanation(s)
  • a: A healthy individualâ€™s skin color on their chest should be uniform and similar to that of the face. Rashes or a change in skin color can occur in relation to allergic reactions, oxygenation issues or circulation issues. Purplish skin around the chest can also be indicative of injury to the lungs or hemorrhage.
  • b: Normal respiratory rate is between 12 and 20 breaths per minute. Respiratory distress due to the inability to ventilate can be caused by lung conditions such as chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) or pneumonia. Breathing slower than this rate is known as hypoventilation and occurs in instances such as sleep apnea or drug overdose.
  • c: The ribs normally slope downward toward the feet at a 45 degree angle in relation to the vertebral column. A more horizontal orientation of the ribs is associated with hyperinflated lungs, otherwise known as barrel chest. This is observed in conditions such as emphysema.

A

B

Sample TCQ (TF)

True or false: When preparing a sterile field from a commercially available sterile pack, the inner surface of the packaging can be used as a sterile field cover.

DOKDifficulty
1Easy

Solution

Answer

True

The answer to a TF question can only be "true" or "false".
Explanation

A sterile field provides a working area for health practitioners that is free of microorganisms. This prevents contamination of surgical tools and medical supplies during their preparation.

A sterile field can be prepared in a number of ways, including from commercially prepackaged kits. During the commercial packaging process, wrapping material is folded over the pack such that four flaps are positioned on the top, outside of the packaging. These flaps provide a place to grab and pull the package open without ever touching the sterile inside of the packaging.

Upon unfolding the flaps, the inner surface of the packaging becomes upward facing. Given that the inner surface of the packaging has not been exposed to any potential contaminants, it serves as the covering of the sterile field. The outer surface of the package has been exposed to the environment and to touching, so it is not sterile.


Hint

Hint: A sterile field is designed to prevent exposing a patient to pathogenic microorganisms.

Sample FIB

An 18-year-old patient enters the emergency room shortly after a skiing accident. The patient exhibits anxiety, dilated pupils, and elevated heart rate. Laboratory testing indicates heightened levels of cortisol in the blood. The patient is most likely in the ____________ stage of general adaptation syndrome.

DOKDifficulty
1Easy

Solution

Answer(s)
  • alarm
Explanation(s)

    General adaptation syndrome (GAS) refers to the three stages of physiological events that follow a physical or psychological stressor. While these events are designed to manage the effect of stressor on the body, a prolonged stress response can be deleterious to health.

    The three stages of GAS are:

  • The alarm stage refers to the immediate response following a stressor. It is typically characterized by a sympatheticâ€”or fight or flightâ€”response, during which a patient experiences increased heart rate and cortisol release for glucose metabolism.
  • The resistance stage occurs after the alarm stage, when the body has adapted to the initial stressor. It is accompanied by restoration of hormone levels and vital signs.
  • Homeostasis refers to the maintenance of stable internal conditions needed for proper functioning. The exhaustion stage occurs if homeostasis is not restored during the resistance stage. It occurs if there are insufficient resources (i.e., energy) for restoration, and it makes the body vulnerable to additional damage.

Sample SA

What are the main differences between hemodialysis and peritoneal dialysis?

DOKDifficulty
2Medium

Solution

Sample Response

During hemodialysis, blood is circulated through a filtering machine that is external to the body. Blood exits the body through an access (i.e., a fistula); filters through the dialysis machine, which removes waste from the blood; then reenters the body. Hemodialysis is a relatively quick (4-6 hours) method done under nursing care.

During peritoneal dialysis, blood remains inside the body. A catheter is used to introduce a detoxifying fluid (i.e., dialysate) into the patientâ€™s abdominal cavity. The peritoneum serves as the filtering membrane through which waste is removed from the blood. The used dialysate fluid and waste are drained out of the body through the catheter. Peritoneal dialysis is often done at the individualâ€™s home and is done over a longer period (6-12 hours).

Explanation

Dialysis is a therapy for individuals with renal disease. Healthy functioning kidneys remove wastes, excess water and solutes, and toxins from the blood. Dialysis performs these functions when the kidneys are not functioning.

Hemodialysis:

  • This process filters blood outside of the body through a dialysis machine containing a filter called a dialyzer.
  • A fistula, graft, or catheter is used to gain access to a blood vessel.
  • Arterial blood is sent from the access to the dialyzer.
  • Blood entering the dialyzer passes through a chamber that is filled with detoxifying dialysate fluid. Dialysate fluid is a bathing solution composed of water and electrolytes.
  • The chambers are separated by a semipermeable membrane. This allows waste (i.e., urea) to collect in the dialysate while maintaining healthy blood composition.
  • Filtered blood is sent from the dialyzer to the access, where it reenters circulation within the body.

Peritoneal dialysis:

  • This process filters blood inside the body using the lining of the abdominal cavity, known as the peritoneum.
  • A catheter is used to gain access to the abdominal cavity.
  • Detoxifying dialysate fluid is introduced into the abdominal cavity through the catheter.
  • The peritoneum is a thin membrane rich with blood vessels. Filling the abdominal cavity with dialysate fluid creates an environment conducive to solute exchange between fluids.
  • Wastes and toxins move from the blood vessels of the peritoneum into the dialysate, and healthy blood composition is maintained.
  • The used dialysate is drained through the catheter along with the waste it contains.


Hint

Hint: Dialysis is used to filter the blood during renal disease.

Sample ES

Describe the additional nutritional needs of a 25-year old when they become pregnant.

DOKDifficulty
2Medium

Solution

Sample Response

A 25-year-old who is pregnant has enhanced nutritional needs that must be met to maintain the needs of both the parent and the developing fetus.

Pregnant individuals must obtain higher levels of energy-yielding nutrients. On average, a pregnant person must consume anywhere between 300 and 500 additional calories a day. This is easily obtained as an additional healthy drink and snack each day.

Throughout pregnancy, it is important to make proper dietary choices. This includes obtaining nutrients from a variety of food groups (fruits, vegetables, grains, dairy, and protein). Pregnant individuals should also minimize consumption of processed foods in favor of whole foods and fresh produce. Finally, people who are pregnant should avoid smoking and consuming alcohol, as these activities introduce toxins to the developing fetus.

Pregnant individuals should receive adequate vitamins and minerals as well. These nutrients are most frequently obtained through diet supplementation with prenatal vitamins. These supplements are enriched with folic acid, vitamins D, iron, and calcium, among other minerals and compounds.

Explanation

Pregnant individuals are encouraged to follow a well-balanced diet with increased daily intake of several major food groups and nutrients:

  • Water intake should increase from ~2 liters to ~3 liters, an increase which is needed for fetal development.
  • Vegetable intake should increase from ~2 cups to ~3 cups to ensure proper vitamin intake.
  • Protein intake should increase to from ~4â€“5 cups to ~6â€“7 cups to support fetal growth.
  • Grain intake should increase from ~6 cups to ~8 cups for increased energy.

Supplements taken by pregnant individuals are enriched with vitamins and minerals needed for proper fetal development. They also ensure that the health of the individual is maintained throughout pregnancy.

  • Folic acid is needed for proper development of the fetal spinal cord.
  • Calcium is needed for proper development of the bones, muscles, and nervous system.
  • Vitamin D helps with absorption of calcium from the diet.
  • Iron supplementation ensures adequate oxygen delivery for both the parent and the fetus.

Sample CALC

A 25 kg patient is prescribed 12.5 mg/kg ampicillin IM. Ampicillin is available in solution at a concentration of 250 mg/mL. What is the appropriate volume of ampicillin solution to be administered to the patient?

DOKDifficulty
2Medium

Solution

Answer

1.25 mL

The answer block should contain the final answer to the calculation (inclusive of units, if applicable). Then provide the step-by-step work that was used to arrive at the answer in the step_by_step block. This should be broken down into logical steps that the student will be able to understand even if they struggle to understand the entire problem.
Explanation
Step 1
The required dose of ampicillin must first be determined from the dosage per weight. This can be done by multiplying the total weight of the patient by the dosage per weight of ampicillin.
Required dose ampicillin = weight patient x dosage per weight
Required dose ampicillin = 25 kg x 12.5 mg/kg
Required dose ampicillin = 312.5 mg
Step 2
The volume of ampicillin to be administered can be calculated from the required dose of ampicillin. Specifically, the required dose of ampicillin can be divided by the concentration of ampicillin in solution.
Volume ampicillin = (required dose ampicillin)/(concentration of ampicillin in solution)
Volume ampicillin = (312.5 mg)/(250 mg/mL)
Volume ampicillin = 1.25 mL

Sample ORD

Arrange the steps of the nursing process in the correct order:
a) implementation
b) diagnosis
c) evaluation
d) assessment
e) planning and outcome identification

DOKDifficulty
1Easy

Solution

Answer

The correct order is: d, b, e, a, c

Explanation

  • Patient assessment is the first part of the nursing process. During the assessment, a nurse collects information pertaining to the patientâ€™s condition.

    For example, a 25-year-old female patient enters the emergency room complaining of dizziness, shortness of breath, and heart palpitations. As part of the patient assessment, the nurse performs a physical examination and notices increased heart rate and decreased skin turgor.

  • During diagnosis, data from the assessment stage is analyzed. This allows for proper identification of patient strengths, along with health concerns that can be addressed through nursing intervention.

    For example, the 25-year-old female patient with increased heart rate and decreased skin turgor is likely experiencing dehydration. The nurse can therefore make a diagnosis of fluid volume deficiency.

  • Planning and outcome identification refers to the process where goals are set to address the indicated health problems. In addition, a plan is set to prevent the health concerns from manifesting again.

    For example, in the 25-year-old patient diagnosed with volume deficiency, establishing fluid and electrolyte balance as the most urgent outcome. The nurse will plan administration of IV therapy, develop a rehydrating nutritional plan, and schedule monitoring of fluid levels.

  • During implementation, the established care plan is executed in order to promote health and wellness. Throughout this process, the nurse should document patient progress and challenges.

    For example, the nurse will administer the 25-year-old patient the appropriate IV, as well as document the amount of food administered and consumed. Further, the nurse will monitor and document urine composition and vital signs according to the established schedule.

  • Evaluation serves to review the effectiveness of the implementation plan. During evaluation, the nurse measures the achievement of established outcomes and identifies variables that impacted the process.

    For example, the ability of the 25-year-old to respond to treatment is evaluated according to vital signs and urine composition. The nurse will note the length of time taken to establish normal vital signs and urine composition, as well as the treatments that were most effective.

Hint(s)

Hint: The nursing process is designed to treat and prevent the recurrence of health problems in a patient.

Sample DRW

Draw a standard electrocardiogram of a healthy adult individual, labeling each of the major waveforms.

DOKDifficulty
1Easy

Solution

Answer
Provide a sample graphic (art asset) and then provide a description of the graphic immediately following the asset. See the Images section for specific instructions on how to generate art assets for solutions. Follow this description with clear instructions directing students how to develop, label, and format their drawings to satisfy the question.


Explanation

An electrocardiogram (ECG) is a non-invasive diagnostic exam used to assess the electrical activity of a patientâ€™s heart. An ECG is conducted by placing several electrodes on the surface of the patientâ€™s skin to record electrical currents through the heart in real time.

An ECG has three main waveforms:

  • The P wave is represented by an initial, slight depolarization from the resting baseline. It occurs upon cation (i.e. Na+ and Ca2+) entry into cardiac muscle cells located within the atria, which corresponds to contraction of the atria of the heart.
  • The QRS segment is represented by a large depolarizing current from the resting baseline. It occurs upon cation (i.e. Na+ and Ca2+) entry into cardiac muscle cells located within the ventricles, which corresponds to electrical activity that is generated when the ventricles contract.
  • The T wave is represented by a depolarization from the resting baseline slightly larger than the P wave. During this process, K+ ions are exiting cardiac muscle cells of the ventricles. Consequently, the cells repolarize. This corresponds to relaxation of the ventricles.

Analysis of the ECG can identify many heart conditions. For example, atrial fibrillation is an irregular heartbeat that increases the risk of stroke. It is characterized by disordered electrical activity within the atria, which can increase the heart rate. On an ECG, atrial fibrillation is identified through the absence of a P wave and prolonged QRS waves.

Sample GR

Drug A and Drug B are compounds being tested for their long-term benefit to HIV+ individuals. A clinical trial was set up using two groups of individuals diagnosed with HIV just prior to the start of the study. Over a four-year period, one group received Drug A and the other group received Drug B. Summarized data for each drug group are presented below (Note: Year 0 corresponds to CD4 count immediately prior to drug treatment).

Average CD4 count (cells/mm3)

Year 0 Year 1 Year 2 Year 3 Year 4
Drug A 195 360 412 450 489
Drug B 202 255 285 292 305

Use the data to plot a graph showing changes in CD4 count over time. Using the graph, state a conclusion regarding the relative effectiveness of the drugs in treating HIV, and justify your conclusion.

DOKDifficulty
2Hard

Solution

Answer

CD4 cells are immune cells specifically targeted by HIV. Therefore, as HIV infection intensifies, CD4 count decreases. As a result, the CD4 cell count can be used as a measure of intensity of the HIV infection. An observed increase in CD4 count upon HIV treatment indicates therapeutic benefit. Consequently, Drug A is more effective at reducing the effects of HIV infection than Drug B.

Explanation
Step 1: Assign the axes the appropriate variables. Specifically, assign the X axis as the independent variable (i.e., year), while the Y axis should be assigned the dependent variable (i.e., CD4 count).
Step 2: Set the bounds of the Y axis. The lower limit can be zero, while the upper limit should be slightly above the highest reported CD4 count. In this case, 500 cells/mm3 is an appropriate value for the upper limit.
Step 3: Plot the average CD4 count at each time point for each drug. Then, connect adjacent data points for Drug A as well as for Drug B. This shows that, while each drug causes CD4 count to rise, Drug A produces a stronger effect.

Sample CL

Classify each medication based on its most frequent route of administration:

a) fluticasone propionate
b) doxorubicin
c) lisinopril
d) tetracyclin
e) amphotericin B
f) beclomethasone


DOKDifficulty
1Medium

Solution

Answer
  • Oral administration: c, d
  • Injection: b, e
  • Inhalation: a, f
Explanation(s)

  • Oral administration refers to taking a medication by mouth. This serves as the most convenient route of administration, posing few risks to the patient. These medications, however, are not maximally absorbed from the digestive tract. Further, upon being absorbed, many of these drugs may be partially metabolized in the liver rather than in the intended target, which prevents maximal activity.

    Common drugs that are administered orally include lisinopril and tetracyclin. Lisinopril is often administered daily and is used to treat hypertension. Tetracyclin is used as an antibiotic, in which case it can often treat urinary tract infections and certain sexually transmitted diseases.

  • Injection refers to the use of a needle to directly introduce a medication into a patient. This can be done by injecting medicine just under the skin (subcutaneous), deep into muscle (intramuscular), or directly into the bloodstream (intravenous). Injection improves the drugâ€™s access to target tissue and prevents the drug from being prematurely metabolized. Despite the benefits, administration by the injection route is less convenient than oral administration, and also poses some risks (i.e. infection).

    Common drugs that are administered via injection include doxorubicin and amphotericin B. Doxorubicin is administered intravenously and serves as a chemotherapeutic. Amphotericin B is also administered intravenously and is used as an antifungal.

  • Inhalation refers to the introduction of a drug into the airway, typically through use of an inhaler. The inhaler contains aerosol droplets of medication within a pressurized container designed for convenient self-administration. During administration, the droplets coat the surface of the recipientâ€™s airway and lungs, causing the drug to diffuse into the bloodstream. This allows the drug to act rapidly.

    Common drugs that are administered via inhalation include fluticasone propionate and beclomethasone. Fluticasone propionate is a steroid medication that is used to treat allergies and other common nasal symptoms (e.g., congestion). Beclomethasone is used for long-term management of asthma as well as treating nasal congestion.
