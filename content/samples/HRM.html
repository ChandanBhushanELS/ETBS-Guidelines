Sample MCQ

Which of the following will help a new employee best understand their attitudes, skills, values and interest?
A. feedback about knowledge and performance
B. goal setting for desired positions
C. gathering data through self-assessment tools
D. action planning and follow-up

DOKDifficulty
1Easy

Solution

Answer

C

Explanation
  • C: Gathering data through self-assessment tools helps employees understand their attitudes, skills, values, and interests. Using the information from the tools as a first step in the career management process lays the foundation for setting realistic career goals.
Incorrect Explanation(s)
  • A: Employers give feedback to employees about their performance and available opportunities after employees have identified the skills they can realistically develop.
  • B: Goal setting occurs after employers give a reality check or feedback in the career development process.
  • D: Creating an action plan and following up on it will occur as a result of the goal setting step in career management.
Hint(s)

Hint 1: Career management begins with an understanding of where an employee is in their career path.
Hint 2: Career management requires a personal and inward focus.

Sample ES

A senior manager observes that a new manager on the team is exhibiting poor conflict management skills and is acting arrogant. Rather than dismissing the new manager, it is determined that employee development should be offered. As an HR representative, you are asked to create an action plan to address the problem.

DOKDifficulty
3Medium

Solution

Sample Response

It is difficult to address problem employees. First, gather information from a variety of sources, in addition to the immediate manager. Be tactful but direct in a conversation with the new manager. Seek to find out how the new manager is doing and attempt to find out what is causing the behavior.

Based on the new managerâ€™s responses, offer the appropriate resources such as training, a referral to an employee assistance plan, counselling, or time off.

Directly provide objective feedback about the behavior using concrete examples and the effect of the behavior on other people. Give examples of appropriate behavior for the new manager to model. Lastly, explain the consequences if there is no behavior change and provide motivation for positive change. This could be accomplished through weekly meetings to review performance changes and assessment of their efforts toward goal achievement.

Explanation

In order to address the issue, a private conversation needs to occur with the new manager. To prepare for the meeting, create an action plan for modifying behavior.
The steps used to create a sample action plan are follows:

  • Gather information to find out what happened by first creating a script of three or four questions to ask the people who are involved. Sample questions include: what happened in the specific instance? Has this happened before? If so, how frequently and how long has the disruptive behavior been happening? Would they like to add anything?

  • Have a meeting with the new manager. Using the list in step 2, have a discussion where the goals are to: dig deeper to find the root of the problem, offer resources and additional training, discuss consequences

  • Provide a written report of what is being specifically required of the new manager to continue employment, and have them acknowledge receipt by signing and dating the report.
  • Follow up with the new manager in a specified time, perhaps three months later, to assess their performance.

    An employee is eligible for overtime pay of time-and-a-half after they work 40 hours in one week. They earn $16 per hour in base pay. If they work 48 hours in a week, what is their total gross pay?

    DOKDifficulty
    1Easy

    Solution

    Answer

    $832

    Explanation
    Step 1
    Calculate the regular weekly pay by multiplying the hourly rate by 40 hours.
    $\begin{aligned}\text{Regular Pay}&=\text{Hourly Rate}\times\text{40 Hours}\\&=\$16\times40\\&=\$640\end{aligned}$
    Step 2
    Calculate the overtime rate by multiplying the regular hourly base rate by 1.5.
    $\begin{aligned}\text{Overtime Rate}&=\text{Regular Base Rate}\times1.5\\&=\$16\times1.5\\&=\$24\end{aligned}$
    Step 3
    Calculate the number of overtime hours by subtracting 40 from the total hours worked.
    $\begin{aligned}\text{Hours Worked Over 40}&=\text{Total Hours Worked}-\text{Regular Work Week Hours}\\&=\text{48 Hours} - \text{40 Hours}\\&=\text{8 Hours}\end{aligned}$
    Step 4
    Calculate the amount of overtime weekly pay by multiplying overtime hours by the overtime rate.
    $\begin{aligned}\text{Overtime Weekly Pay}&=\text{Overtime Hours}\times\text{Overtime Rate}\\&=\text{8 Hours}\times\text{\$24 Per Hour}\\&=\$192\end{aligned}$
    Step 5
    Calculate the total weekly pay.
    $\begin{aligned}\text{Total Weekly Pay}&=\text{Regular Pay} + \text{Overtime Pay}\\&=\$640+\$192\\&=\$832\end{aligned}$

    Sample ORD

    Order the five steps in the instructional design process.
    1. development (create the learning materials)
    2. design (determine learning objectives)
    3. implementation (deliver information to target audience)
    4. evaluation (use assessment measurement to determine effectiveness of training)
    5. assessment (determine needs and resources)

    DOKDifficulty
    1Easy

    Solution

    Answer

    The correct order is: 5, 2, 1, 3, 4

    Explanation
    • An assessment of training needs is the first step where the organization decides what the employees need to learn.
    • Next, the organization must determine if the employees have the necessary skills, attitudes, and motivations in order to begin the training.
    • Planning for the training includes deciding on the learning objectives for the training, finding the instructors, and deciding on the appropriate training measure.
    • After planning, the training is implemented. The training classes take place.
    • The results of the training are evaluated. This step provides feedback on how well the training met its stated objectives..
    Hint (s)

    Hint 1: The order of the instructional design process is similar to the decision-making process.
    Hint 2: A feedback loop should be designed into the system in order to make it dynamic.
    Hint 3: Before any progress can occur in the design function, the process must begin with a definition of purpose and scope.

    Sample MAT

    Match the function of human resources with the description of its role.

    1. recruiting
    2. training
    3. interviewing
    4. selecting

    a. the process of questioning potential applicants
    b. the process of choosing the ideal candidate
    c. the process of sourcing candidates for a specific role
    d. the process of preparing new employees for the job
    DOKDifficulty
    1Easy

    Solution

    Answer
    • c
    • d
    • a
    • b
    Explanation
    • c: During the recruiting function, the focus is to gather as many qualified applicants for the open position as possible.
    • d: During the training function, the focus is to onboard newly hired employees and prepare them for their new roles.
    • a: During the interviewing function, the focus is to narrow candidates down through a series of questions and to assess their fit with the organization.
    • b: During the selecting function, the focus is to choose a candidate from the pool of potential applicants.
    Hint(s)

    Hint 1: Recruiting involves attracting people for jobs.

    Hint 2: Training involves a series of chronological steps that a candidate follows to understand and perform the role.

    Hint 3: Interviewing involves a formal meeting in which one person asks questions and another provides answers.

    Hint 4: Selecting involves eliminating unqualified candidates.
