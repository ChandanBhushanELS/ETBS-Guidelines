Determine if Art is Needed

The only time art is required in a solution is for the Draw (DRW) question type. These are questions that ask a student to "Draw a ____" , "Sketch a ____", "Construct a ____", so the expected output is visual.

Additionally, art is sometimes required in Graph (GR) question types depending on subject area. For example, some economics textbooks may ask students to draw a graph.

Rarely, art can be used to supplement explanations in solutions in other question types; however, there must be a clear and necessary benefit to using the art over a textual explanation.

NOTE: JSXGraphs created for math textbooks are not considered art.

Determine What Type of Art to Include

There are two types of art that can be used when developing solutions for Evelyn Learning.


Art Type Definition Created By
1. Evelyn Learning Pickup Existing CH art from the Art Library (DAM); used as is without modifications Already exists

Art Type 1 - Evelyn Learning Pickup

Evelyn Learning Pickup art is "picked up" from our Art Library of existing art assets and inserted into solutions as is. These assets were originally created for our Study Guide project.




Adding Evelyn Learning Art to a Solution:
  1. Access the Evelyn Learning NetX portal DAM.
  2. Search the Art Library for the image needed. TIP: Use keywords related to the content to more quickly find images; however, some images may not be tagged, so always look through the Courses in the DAM as well.
  3. Once you have found the image you want, click the image ("View asset") to view the image detail page.

  4. Copy the "Asset ID" from the details pageâ€”this is usually a 4-digit number.
  5. In the Authoring Tool, click into the field where you would like to add the image to make the Text Formatting Toolbar appear.
  6. Choose "Insert DAM Image" and paste the Asset ID into the popup box that appears.
  7. Click "OK."
  8. Click on the "Preview" icon and confirm if the Authoring Tool is showing the correct art image.

Art Type 2 - Author Created: Hand-Drawn Art

When Should I Use Hand-drawn Art?

Hand-drawn art should be used when an appropriate asset cannot be found in the Evelyn Learning Art Library (DAM) and in cases when the Evelyn Learning provided templates cannot be used.


Hand-drawn Art Guidelines

  • Draw with black pen on blank, white paper
  • Write letters with clear handwriting
  • Avoid calligraphy or complex writing styles
  • Use a ruler or straight edge when drawing lines
  • Keep your drawings clean for students to easily understand them

Transfer Your Hand-Drawn Art to Your Computer

OPTION 1 (Recommended): Use the Scanner Pro App
  1. Download the Scanner Pro by Readdle from the Apple App Store or Android Store (around $3.99).
  2. Draw your art according to the hand-drawn art guidelines (see above).
  3. Open your Scanner Pro app on your phone.
  4. Tap the orange "+" button and follow the on screen instructions to capture a photo of your art.
  5. After the photo is taken, click the blue box in the lower right corner. It should indicate the number of photos that you took.
  6. If needed, crop the image into a nice clean file using the Edit mode. Alternatively, you can try to take another photo.
  7. Click on the file name directly below the image and rename the file — it will currently be named as the date and time you took the photo.
    Format: [Question ID]-[description]
    Example: 00H0TE-venn-diagram
  8. Click the "Share" button under the image.
  9. To the left of "Share As:" update the file type to JPEG and make sure the file size is set to "Large."
  10. Use the "Share" options to send the file to your computer.
    Send to your Evelyn Learning email and download the attachment to your computer then add to Google Drive.
    Send directly to Google Drive using mobile Google Drive app to upload immediately to your personal Google Drive.
OPTION 2: Use a Photo Scanner
  1. Put your hand-drawn art in a scanner and scan with high-resolution quality (600 dpi).
  2. Capture the file and save it as .jpg.
  3. Crop the file as needed, don't leave too much white space around the edges and ensure no other background is visible.
  4. Rename the file and save to your desktop.
    Format: [Question ID]-[Description]
    Example: 00H0TE-venn-diagram
After Downloading the File Check the File Size
For Macs:
  1. Right click on the downloaded image file on your desktop.
  2. Choose "Get Info."
  3. Look for "Dimensions" under the "More Info" section of the popup.
  4. Confirm the dimension are great than 700px.
Upload the Source File into Google Drive
  1. Open Google Chrome and navigate to 4 TQ Draft Files folder of the Textbook Question Solution Authoring shared Google Drive.
  2. Find your textbook's folder and then open the images folder for your textbook.
  3. Drag the downloaded image from your desktop to the Google Drive Image folder to upload it.
  4. Confirm the image uploaded correctly by clicking on it to preview.

Setting Up Your Basic Charts and Graphs Working File
  1. Access the Textbook Question Solution Authoring shared Google Drive using your Evelyn Learning email (gmail) account.
  2. Find the folder for your textbook in 4 TQ Draft Files on the Textbook Question Solution Authoring Drive.
  3. Find the Images folder within your textbook folderâ€”if chapter subfolders exist, make sure to check for your image folder there.
  4. Click on the "+ New" at the top-left of the screen.
    • Hover over Google Sheets.
    • Hover over the arrow to the right.
    • Choose "From a template".
  5. Choose "TQ Basic Charts and Graphs TEMPLATE".
  6. Name your newly created file with the Textbook Code, Chapter Name, and Your Name.
    Format: [TextbookCode]-[ChapterNumber]-[YourName]
    Example: 00L-Chapter5-JaneDoe
Creating Your Basic Charts and Graphs Content
  1. Duplicate the tab of the chart you'd like to use.
  2. Name the new tab with the Question ID.
  3. Edit the data, adding/deleting rows and columns as needed.
  4. Do NOT change the colors, fonts or other design elements of the chart or graph. It will update automatically as you edit the data in the sheet.
Create, Name and Upload the SVG File into Google Drive
  1. Click on the chart/graph you created.
  2. Click on the 3 dots at the top right of the chart/graph.
  3. Choose Download > Scalable Vector Graphics (.svg).
  4. Find the downloaded SVG and name using the guidelines below. All names must:
    • Start with the question ID
    • Be descriptive of the art content
    • Use all lowercase letters
    • Use dashes between words
    • Include the .svg file extension
    • Do NOT use:
      Spaces in the filename
      Special characters
      ~ ! @ # $ % ^ & * ( ) ` ; < > ? , [ ] { } ' "
    • Examples
      00H0TE-venn-diagram.svg
      00J080-line-graph.svg
  5. Upload your SVG to the correct images folder for your textbook.

Adding Art to Draw (DRW) Questions

  1. Click on the "My Computer" below the "Upload SVG Image from".



  2. Confirm if the file has been uploaded successfully and if it is showing correctly using the "Preview" feature.
  3. If the art was created using a Google Sheet Template, include the link to your Google Sheet in the "General Comments" section in the Authoring Tool.
  4. Tick the "This solution contains modified or new art" checkbox below the Taxonomy field.
