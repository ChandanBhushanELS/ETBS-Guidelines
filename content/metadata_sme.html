DOK Level

Depth of Knowledge (DOK) is a framework for determining educational goals and objectives. For our purposes, it categorizes the level of thinking required to answer a question. The scale runs from 1 to 4 as follows:

  1. Recall/Reproduction - Requires a student to simply state or understand a fact or concept. This may include providing a definition, example, or summary. It can also include explaining a concept or how concepts relate, solving examples, etc.
  2. Skills/Concepts - Requires a student to apply their knowledge of a concept to a specific situation.
  3. Strategic Thinking - Requires a student to break down underlying concepts and understand the organization of ideas or relationships between ideas. It can also require a student to evaluate an example or implementation of a concept.
  4. Extended Thinking - Requires a student to create a new, novel work.

Difficulty

Difficulty represents relatively how easy or hard a student taking the relevant course would find the question. All titles/chapters should have a range of difficulties. Keep in mind that this is a relative measure within the book, not absolute.

  1. Easy - Answering the question should be trivial if the student has read the material.
  2. Medium - Answering the question requires a reasonable amount of effort and success would represent an appropriate level of knowledge.
  3. Hard - Most students would be expected to struggle to answer the question, or require multiple attempts. Success would demonstrate mastery of the concept.

  • In general, there is no need to include concepts that are the same as the chapter or section title for the solution. These will be included automatically.
