TQ Premium Quality Rubric

Key Features of Quality Solutions
  • 100% accurate
  • Original work in the SME’s own words
  • Clear of grammar and mechanical issues
  • Effective, specific, and concise
  • Use precise subject matter terminology
  • Include necessary steps or background information to arrive at the correct answer
  • Avoid superficial explanations
  • Written at the appropriate level of content knowledge for the given course or textbook chapter
  • Do not repeat language from the question prompt or question options
TQ Solution Sections

Solutions consist of multiple sections that vary depending on the question type.

Answer

Correct response that satisfies the prompt. Answers are often multiple choice options, written responses, numerical values, or visual assets.

Explanation

Explanation of why an answer is correct. The explanation provides supporting evidence for a correct answer or shows steps needed to arrive at the answer.

Wrong Explanation

Explanation of why an answer is incorrect. The explanation provides factual evidence that disproves the incorrect answer choice. The explanation does not cue the correct response.

Sample Response

Written response to an open-ended question that may have more than one correct answer. The sample response should show the background information or steps needed to provide a complete response.

Step-by-step

Description of a process or calculation that includes necessary intermediate steps and provides rationales for why each step is included. Step-by-steps should always have at least two steps and a maximum of eight steps.

MCQ/MS/TF/FIB Acceptance Criteria:
  • Solution is correct and complete.
  • Writing is original work in the SME’s own words.
  • The writing is free of errors.
  • The explanation is clear and explains why the answer choice is correct.
  • Each wrong explanation is clear and explains why the answer choice is incorrect.
  • Explanations are succinct, but provide enough detail to aid in student understanding.
  • Explanations do not reference each other and do not cue each other.
  • Explanations do not make direct reference to answer option letters.

  • Writing is original work in the SME’s own words.
  • The writing is free of errors.
  • The sample response clearly states a correct answer or begins with a clear thesis statement.
  • Sample response is succinct, but provides enough detail to aid in student understanding.
  • The explanation provides necessary background information to arrive at the correct answer.
  • Explanation is succinct, but provides enough detail to aid in student understanding.

  • The writing is free of errors.
  • Explanations are succinct, but provide enough detail to aid in student understanding.
  • Explanations provide necessary background information to arrive at the correct answer.

CALC/GR Acceptance Criteria:
  • Solution is correct and complete.
  • Writing and/or asset are original work in the SME’s own words and depictions.
  • The writing and/or asset are free of errors.
  • Steps are broken down into logical chunks and there are two to eight steps included.
  • Each step includes both the description and the computation for the step.
  • Descriptions explain why each step is the logical in sequence and provide information required to show the computation.
  • Computations include the general form of any formulas needed to solve the step and include intermediate steps.
  • Graphs are generated using JSXGraph and code is well formed.
  • Formulas and equations are generated using KaTeX and code is well formed.
DRW/LDGR Acceptance Criteria:
  • Solution is correct and complete.
  • Writing and asset are original work in the SME’s own words and depictions.
  • The writing and asset are free of errors.
  • Appropriate labels are provided for student understanding.
  • Explanations or steps are succinct, but provide enough detail to aid in student understanding.
  • The explanation provides necessary background information to arrive at the correct answer.

Characteristics of High-Quality Solutions

Learning & Comprehension
  • Show student how, don’t just give answer
  • Explain necessary background information or steps to find answer
  • Demonstrate critical thinking used to answer question
Style/Formatting
  • Use bullets to format lists
  • Clearly label steps for step-by-step solutions
  • Provide short, concise explanations
  • Directly address each part of a question
Grammar/Syntax
  • Free from grammatical mistakes
  • Sentence structure is clear and natural sounding
  • English American Vernacular
